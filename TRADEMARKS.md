## What this means in practice

- use, modify and redistribute the code, including commercially, under the MIT terms
- fork the project and publish your fork **under a different name**
- state factually that your project is "based on GleeLight" or "a fork of GleeLight"

- call your fork, app, or product "GleeLight", or use a name confusingly similar to it

The point is simply that users should always know whose build they are installing. If you
build something on top of GleeLight, give it your own name — the code is yours to use.
